Core Legal Rights of Women

1. Right to File a Zero FIR

A victim of a crime can file an FIR at any police station, irrespective of where the incident occurred. The police cannot refuse registration, and must subsequently transfer the FIR to the jurisdictional police precinct.

2. Section 46 of CrPC

Guarantees that a woman cannot be arrested after sunset and before sunrise except under exceptional circumstances, and only by a female police officer who has obtained judicial magistrate clearance beforehand.

3. Right to Identity Protection

Under Section 228-A of IPC, it is a punishable offense to publish or disclose the identity of a victim of sexual assault, preserving privacy throughout investigation processes.

4. Right to Free Legal Assistance

Women are entitled to free legal aid through state/national legal services organizations (NALSA/SALSA), regardless of income bracket or crime category.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Voice Activated SOS function?
When enabled, SafeHer leverages the browser's Web Speech API implementation to continuously process speech inputs. Recognizable distress triggers immediately engage the active SOS alert protocol without needing app interactions.
Are my coordinates shared with the public?
Absolutely not. Location coordinates are processed locally and securely dispatched only to your designated trusted contacts network. SafeHer guarantees privacy compliance; coordinates are never saved publicly.
Will the audio recorder work if my phone screen is locked?
Audio recording is managed inside standard HTML5 scopes. As long as the browser processes active tabs (background processes allowed), the microphone stream captures threat sounds securely.
How is the Route Safety Level determined?
Our AI recommendation algorithm evaluates routes using static safety attributes (e.g. lit street lamp coverage, proximity to hospitals/stations, historical records, and crowd estimates at selected hours) to classify transit paths into low, moderate, or high risk zones.
